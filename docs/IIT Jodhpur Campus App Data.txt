Comprehensive Digital and Physical Infrastructure Audit: Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur (IITJ)
The following analysis provides an exhaustive architectural, operational, and institutional mapping of the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur (IITJ). Engineered to supply a structured data foundation for a centralized campus application, this report synthesizes the latest available institutional records, administrative circulars, and student-led digital infrastructure datasets up to the 2026 academic operating environment. The data is structured to inform the database schema, user experience (UX) flows, and API integrations for a centralized campus mobile application.
1. Dining Infrastructure and Mess Menu Systems
The dining infrastructure at IIT Jodhpur operates under a centralized mess system designed to cater to a fully residential student body across an 852-acre campus. The operational architecture is bifurcated to strictly maintain dietary preferences, cultural requirements, and hygiene standards1.
The institute operates two primary mess facilities situated adjacent to the hostel blocks. The Vegetarian Mess operates as a dedicated facility strictly for vegetarian food preparation and consumption1. The Non-Vegetarian Mess is a separate facility featuring independent preparation areas, where students can opt for a non-vegetarian or specialized paneer-based item daily, which is typically provisioned in limited quantities3. In addition to the primary mess facilities, a multi-cuisine Food Court operates on the third floor of the Dining Hall building. This venue functions on a pay-per-item basis, offering a la carte menus, fresh juices, and late-night culinary services beyond standard mess operating hours2.
The operational windows for the mess strictly adhere to a four-meal daily structure synchronized with the academic and laboratory timetable. Based on historical and current operational blueprints, the meal distribution follows a precise schedule1.

	5. Campus Geography and Asset Locations
IIT Jodhpur occupies a massive 852-acre footprint situated along National Highway 62 (Nagaur Road, Karwar). The topography is strategically divided into Pocket A (650 acres, hosting the Main Academic and Residential complexes) and Pocket B (192 acres, housing the Technology Park and KV School)8.
A defining architectural feature of the campus is its 5-Star GRIHA (Green Rating for Integrated Habitat Assessment) sustainability rating. The design treats the settlement as a "Living Laboratory," utilizing massive earthen "berms" to shield the infrastructure from the harsh desert heat, dust storms, and noise36.
The academic architecture deliberately avoids traditional departmental isolation to foster interdisciplinary research. Academic blocks are named after foundational Indian scientists8.

	3. Academic Calendar and Educational Chronology (AY 2026-27)
IIT Jodhpur employs a highly innovative academic framework structured around a "Fractal" system. This paradigm subdivides traditional semester-long courses into smaller, intensive modular blocks (Fractal 1, 2, and 3), allowing for unparalleled flexibility and cross-disciplinary exploration14. This unique structure fundamentally alters standard drop/add deadlines and examination windows, demanding precise integration into any student-facing application.
The academic session for 2026-27 is segmented into three primary operational windows: Semester I (Monsoon), Semester II (Spring), and a Summer Term14.
For Semester I, online course registration for continuing students opens between 20 and 23 July 202614. The physical reporting and document verification for new undergraduate cohorts occurs closely thereafter, from 27 to 29 July 202614. Instructional classes commence for continuing students on 30 July 2026, while first-year undergraduates begin their academic journey on 05 August 202614. The critical deadline for late registration and the general add/drop of courses is scheduled for 07 August 202614. However, due to the fractal structure, specific withdrawal deadlines are staggered; for instance, the withdrawal deadline for Fractal 1 courses is 31 August 2026, and for Fractal 2 courses, it is 07 October 202614. Minor examinations, serving as mid-semester evaluations, run from 15 to 20 September 2026, followed by the rigorous Major examinations culminating the semester between 19 and 26 November 202614.
Semester II initiates with online registration from 15 to 17 December 2026, leading into class commencement on 04 January 202714. The add/drop window closes rapidly on 07 January 202714. Minor examinations for this spring session are calendared for 16 to 21 February 2027, with the final Major examinations stretching from 22 to 29 April 202714.
The institute's operational rhythm is punctuated by several major holidays and officially declared "No Class Days" that accommodate campus-wide cultural and technical festivals. Significant pauses include Republic Day (26 January), Holi (04 March), Independence Day (15 August), and Diwali (08 November)14. Crucially for application scheduling, instructional activities are suspended for the Prometeo technical festival (02–04 October 2026), the Varchas sports festival (30 October–01 November 2026), and the Ignus cultural festival (29–31 January 2027)14.
A minor discrepancy exists within the documentation regarding the annual Convocation. While the 10th Convocation occurred in October 2024, the 11th is noted for June 2025, and subsequent brochures suggest the 12th Convocation will occur between 04 and 06 June 202618.

	4. Digital Portals and Access Architecture
The digital ecosystem at IIT Jodhpur is engineered and maintained by the Digital Infrastructure & Automation (DIA) vertical20. The architecture heavily relies on secure, authenticated gateways to protect sensitive academic and administrative data. Understanding the underlying authentication protocols is vital for seamless app integration.
The primary Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform functions as the central student portal. Located at https://erp.iitj.ac.in/, this portal manages course registration, fee status, and academic records24. Accessing the ERP from an external network requires a dynamic One-Time Password (OTP) routed to the student's registered mobile device or email, adding a layer of friction for third-party app scraping21. However, access initiated from within the institute's internal network bypasses this OTP requirement21. The ERP portal also natively integrates the Hostel Booking interface and the institutional Fee Payment gateways, centralizing financial transactions once administrative approvals are granted25.
The Learning Management System (LMS) is powered by Moodle and is accessible at https://moodle.iitj.ac.in/26. Authentication utilizes the institute's Lightweight Directory Access Protocol (LDAP), meaning students log in using the prefix of their official @iitj.ac.in email address29. Student webmail is hosted externally through Google Workspace, accessible via standard Gmail interfaces using the full institutional email address30.
The S. R. Ranganathan Learning Hub (SRRLH) manages the library's digital catalog at https://library.iitj.ac.in/27. While the public catalog is openly accessible, remote access to subscribed research databases necessitates logging into the RemoteXs proxy server at http://iitj.remotexs.in/user using LDAP credentials29. Furthermore, the Training and Placement Office (TPO) operates the Student Placement Cell (SPC) portal at https://spc.iitj.ac.in/, where students must securely authenticate to upload resumes for verification and apply for corporate opportunities32.
Network access represents a significant infrastructural layer. The campus is blanketed by a high-speed WPA-2 Enterprise wireless network supported by robust leased lines from NKN, BSNL, PGCIL, and Airtel23. Accessing the internet requires navigating a Fortinet captive portal (gateway.iitj.ac.in or 10.0.0.1 equivalents) using LDAP credentials34. Due to the short session timeouts enforced by the FortiGate firewall, students frequently rely on custom scripts or browser extensions (such as the "IIT J Gateway Extension") to automate re-authentication66. Furthermore, accessing internal administrative nodes or software license servers from off-campus requires the installation of the FortiClient SSL-VPN software22.

	5. Topographical Infrastructure and Campus Mapping
IIT Jodhpur occupies a massive 852-acre footprint on National Highway 62 in Karwar2. The topography is master-planned to function as an ecologically sustainable "Living Laboratory," meticulously bifurcated into two primary zones: Pocket A (650 acres) encompassing the main academic and residential complexes, and Pocket B (192 acres) dedicated to the Technology Park and educational facilities like the Kendriya Vidyalaya8.
The architectural design is a direct response to the harsh Thar Desert environment. The perimeter and internal zones utilize massive earthen "berms" to mitigate acoustic pollution, thermal loading, and dust infiltration37. This design philosophy has earned the campus a prestigious 5-Star rating from the Green Rating for Integrated Habitat Assessment (GRIHA) Council36.
To foster interdisciplinary synergies, the academic infrastructure avoids traditional departmental clustering. Instead, monumental academic blocks are named in honor of pioneering Indian scientists8:
* Administrative Hub: The Chanakya Complex, housing the Director's Office and central administration8.
* Physics: Bibha Chowdhuri Bhawan8.
* Bioscience & Bioengineering: Asima Chatterjee Bhawan8.
* Civil & Infrastructure Engineering: A. Ramakrishna Bhawan8.
* Metallurgy, Materials & Chemical Engineering: T.R. Anantharaman Bhawan8.
* Computer Centre & IT: Srinivasa Ramanujan Centre8.
* Artificial Intelligence & Data Science: P.C. Mahalanobis Bhawan8.
* Innovation & Incubation: APJ Abdul Kalam Innovation Complex8.
* Central Library: S. R. Ranganathan Learning Hub (SRRLH)8.
The residential sector is equally expansive, comprising 17 distinct hostel blocks (12 for male students and 5 for female students) capable of accommodating over 3,000 individuals8. The nomenclature of these hostels blends alphanumeric designations with the names of resilient desert flora42:
* Female Hostels: Hostel 12, Hostel 13, B2, B4 (Taman), G4 (Shirish), and Aparajita (I2)42.
* Male Hostels: G1, G2, G3 (Sheesham), G5, G6 (Arjun), B1 (Neelmohar), B3 (Semal), B5, Y3, Y4, O3, and O442.
The newer additions, such as Hostel Gulmohar, were constructed using rapid pre-cast technology8. Notably, the rooms are engineered with advanced thermal insulation and centralized air-cooling systems, rendering individual ACs or desert coolers unnecessary8.
Crucial secondary facilities are interspersed throughout the residential zones. The Jivaka Primary Health Centre (PHC) provides centralized medical support, while a dedicated sports complex offers extensive athletic fields alongside indoor gymnasiums within the individual hostel blocks4.
